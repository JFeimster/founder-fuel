revenue based financing for startups

Revenue-Based Financing

A financing path tied to revenue performance, often used by ecommerce or service businesses with consistent deposits, processor data, and a clear growth use case.

Apply for a reality checkBack to all options
Who it fits

Works better when revenue is real, visible, and steady enough to support repayment.

Revenue-based financing can be a fit for founders with actual traction who need speed, but it requires sober math. It is not a cure for weak margins or unstable cash flow.

  • Consistent revenue and processor or bank statement history
  • A use of funds connected to measurable growth
  • Gross margin strong enough to absorb repayment drag

What founders like

  • Can move faster than traditional lenders.
  • Often fits ecommerce and digital service businesses better than banks do.
  • Uses traction signals instead of waiting for years of history.

What founders underestimate

  • Repayment pressure can choke cash flow if growth assumptions are soft.
  • Founders often underestimate how fast capital disappears when margins are thin.
  • Expensive capital used for weak offers usually compounds the problem.
